## ⚙️ Setting Up Your First Connection

After you start the application, look for a small icon in your system tray. This icon represents the autofuse manager. Follow these actions to add your first remote folder:

1. Click the autofuse icon to open the main menu.
2. Select the option labeled "Add New Connection."
3. Enter a label for your connection so you recognize it later.
4. Type the address of your remote server.
5. Provide your username for that server.
6. Choose the folder where you want your remote files to appear on your computer.
7. Click the save button to confirm these settings. 

Your connection now appears in the menu list. Click it whenever you want to see your remote files.

## 🔍 Troubleshooting Common Issues

Check these items if you experience trouble with your connections.

**The connection stays grey or inactive.**
Verify your internet connection. Ensure the remote server is powered on and reachable. If you use a Wake-on-LAN feature, verify that the remote computer has this setting enabled in its power settings.

**Files appear empty or do not load.**
Refresh the connection by selecting "Disconnect" and then "Connect" from the menu. Check that your user credentials have the permissions to read the files on the remote machine.

**The application does not start.**
Ensure your computer satisfies all minimum system requirements. Restart your computer and attempt to launch the application again. If the issue remains, remove the software and download a fresh copy from the link above.

*   Review your active connections list regularly and remove any that you no longer need.

## 📋 Ongoing Management

The menu bar icon changes color to show your status. Green indicates that your connections are active and ready. Yellow means the software is attempting to reconnect. Red suggests an error that requires your attention. 

Click the icon to see specific error logs if a connection fails. These logs tell you exactly why a server connection reached a limit or failed to authenticate. You can clear these logs to keep your interface clean after you solve the underlying issue. 

If you prefer to keep your menu bar clear, you can hide the icon in the application settings. Access your connections later through the taskbar search or by opening the main application window directly from your desktop.
